Earth

Earth – our home planet – is the third planet from the Sun, and the fifth largest planet. It's the only place we know of inhabited by living things.


Earth's History

Earth formed 4.6 billion years ago from a collapsing cloud of gas and dust, creating the Sun and planets. In its early years, it was a fiery, molten world, bombarded by asteroids and intense volcanism. Around 4.5 billion years ago, a collision with a Mars-sized body, Theia, led to the Moon's formation. As Earth cooled, a crust formed, volcanic steam created an atmosphere, and by 4.4 billion years ago, the first oceans appeared—setting the stage for life.
